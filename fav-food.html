Jollof Spaghetti

Origin of Spaghetti

Spaghetti comes from the word Spago, which translates in English to “string,” or “twine.” in Italy, spaghetti (like all pasta) are generally cooked just to al dente (which means “to the tooth.”) to create a slightly chewy texture, rather than an overly soft consistency. Jollof Spaghetti is an African recipe for spaghetti.

Spaghetti is popularly said to originate from italy. National spaghetti day is January 4th ( Although we doubt you need a special holiday to enjoy this meal).

Recipe for 4 portions:  

Ingredient Serving
Spaghetti 400g
Onions(Chopped) 1
Fresh Pepper 1.35
Fresh Tomatoes 2.65
Red Bell Pepper 1.35 large
Chopped garlic 0.35tsp
Vegetable oil 1.35tbsp
Curry Powder 0.65 tsp
Thyme 0.35tsp
Chopped ginger 0.35 tsp
Carrots(Diced) 1.35 Mediums
Cabbage 0.65 Head
Green Bell Pepper 1.35

Instructions

  1. Wash and roughly blend tomatoes, 1 onion,fresh pepper and 1 red bell pepper. Cut remaining red and green bell pepper into a small bowl then set aside.
  2. Slice remaining onions.Heat oil in a pan and add onion slices,ginger,garlic,thyme then stir fry for 3 minutes.
  3. Add the blended vegetables and fry for 10 minutes stirring at regular intervals.
  4. Add the spaghetti,stir and allow to cook on low heat for 7 minutes.
  5. Shred the cabbage, the red and green bell peppers and diced carrots.
  6. Add all to the pan and Stir at regular interval till spaghetti is cooked.
  7. Serve and Enjoy!
